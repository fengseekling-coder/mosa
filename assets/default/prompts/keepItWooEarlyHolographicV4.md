## Three-Level Architectural Structure

### 2. Monumental circular gate mechanism

Place one true circle at approximately 46% of canvas height. Its diameter should occupy 72%–78% of canvas width, making it the dominant architectural object without becoming a logo.

Build the circle from seven clearly differentiated vector layers, each with its own purpose and spacing:

1. An outer halo contour: two near-complete ultra-thin silver arcs with precise breaks at the top, bottom, and central seam.
2. A structural shell: two mirrored translucent graphite crescent plates, each divided into three wide radial sectors with crisp chamfered ends.
3. A calibration track: one ring of sparse short bars and dots, grouped into four balanced clusters rather than evenly repeated around the entire circumference.
4. A topological membrane: 18–24 fine parallel contour paths inside each half, bending continuously toward the central gap, locally compressing near the opening edge and expanding toward the outside. Lines must never cross or tangle.
5. A floating data layer: six to eight semi-transparent vector arcs with subtle 2D offsets, creating depth through overlap and parallax while preserving front-facing geometry.
6. An inner aperture ring: one precise thin circle with four discreet registration nodes and two narrow breaks aligned to the seam.
7. A central void: a deep-black circular aperture with no icon, logo, lightning bolt, target, or sphere—only a tiny controlled white activation point at the exact center.

Use alternating transparency and tonal values so each of the seven layers is visually separable. The structure should feel like stacked optical films, laser-cut acrylic graphics, or an advanced printed interface, but remain clearly designed as vector artwork.

### 3. Central opening seam

- Above the circle: two parallel hairlines separated by a narrow black channel.
- Through the circle: widen the black channel so the two circular halves visibly part by 3%–5% of the circle diameter.
- At the center: create the deepest negative-space gap and a subtle inward taper, suggesting spatial depth.
- Below the circle: allow the seam to narrow again and connect into the typography grid.

The left and right circular halves should have slightly staggered transparent layers, as if deeper planes are opening a fraction of a second later than the front planes. Keep all offsets mirrored and mathematically controlled. This layered lag is essential to the “open sesame” feeling.
